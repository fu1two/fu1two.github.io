Conflict

Strong Personalities

5.31.15

For this example I’ll go back to a class of mine back in graduate school. This was a practicum class run by a design firm that my school partnered with. Within this class we were split into teams. I was the only male on my team. There were two very strong personalities on my team and I would clash with them indirectly on occasion. The conflict was never verbal, but more of them dismissing all my ideas and most of what I would say during our group meetings. It was basically their way or nothing. I had put a lot of effort into an idea for a part of our final project and it was essentially dismissed. The girl who led our group sidestepped around it completely. It was an awful feeling to say the least.

The source of the conflict, or potential conflict, was that I didn’t speak up enough and she spoke up too much. And ignored my thoughts the few times I did speak up. I felt disappointed more than anything else. I wasn’t really mad. My ideas being dismissed made me feel saddened more than anything. A lack of purpose is the worst feeling ever. And when your ideas are never incorporated it often feels like that.

I resolved the conflict internally within myself. I spoke to another member of my group about how I felt. She too stated that she often felt that her ideas were dismissed by the two stronger personalities. It helped to know that I wasn’t in that boat alone. In order to solve it with the least resistance, I simply put my efforts into what they assigned me of their ideas. I figured if I wasn’t going to be able to work on my own suggestions, then I should at least do the best I can with what I am given.

If I could go back I would definitely speak up more. I would be sterner about my ideas and not fall back on my heels so easily. I learned that the path of least resistance is not always the best path or correct.
